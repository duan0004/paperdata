\title{Calibrated PTA Evidence Identifies Low-Frequency Curvature but Not a Unique Nanohertz Source}

\author{Ran DUAN}
\email{duanran@nao.cas.cn}
\affiliation{National Astronomical Observatories, Chinese Academy of Sciences}
\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
The nanohertz gravitational-wave background admits both astrophysical
and cosmological interpretations.  We test whether current PTA
Bayesian evidence remains source-discriminating once template
provenance, density construction, and curved-SMBHB controls are matched.
Official PTArcade templates and official \texttt{ceffyl} densities
reproduce the published NANOGrav 15-year log-Bayes factors for
SIGW-Gaussian, SIGW-delta, and cosmic superstrings at sub-nat agreement.
On that calibrated scale, one physical environmental-SMBHB control and
two predeclared phenomenological low-frequency curvature surrogates yield
$\ln\mathcal{B}=3.45$--$3.84$.  The best curvature surrogate lies
$0.681$ nat below SIGW-Gaussian, while the physical environmental control
lies $1.067$ nat below SIGW-Gaussian and $0.476$ nat below SIGW-delta,
comparable to identified calibration and prior-boundary systematics.  The
phenomenological rows are null tests of low-frequency curvature, not
population-synthesis predictions.  A public cross-PTA posterior-summary
bridge preserves the same order-unity compression.  Current public PTA
spectral evidence identifies low-frequency curvature more robustly than a
unique source class.
\end{abstract}

\maketitle

\emph{Introduction.}
Pulsar timing arrays have opened the nanohertz gravitational-wave
window.  The NANOGrav 15-year data set reports a common red process with
Hellings--Downs spatial correlations \cite{NANOGrav_15yr_GWB}.  The
companion noise analysis \cite{NANOGrav_15yr_noise} provides the
white-noise budget used in the public analyses.  Comparable public PTA
evidence for a nanohertz background is available from PPTA DR3
\cite{PPTA_DR3} and EPTA+InPTA DR2 \cite{EPTA_InPTA_DR2}.  The signal
is compatible with an astrophysical supermassive-black-hole-binary
(SMBHB) background \cite{Phinney_2001} and with several cosmological
sources.
The NANOGrav
new-physics search \cite{NANOGrav_15yr_NewPhysics}, using PTArcade
\cite{PTArcade_2023}, reports strong-but-not-decisive Bayes factors
relative to the SMBHB baseline for several leading stochastic
alternatives, including SIGW-Gaussian ($\mathcal{B}=57$), SIGW-delta
($\mathcal{B}=44$), and cosmic superstrings ($\mathcal{B}=46$),
placing these models in the strong-but-not-decisive evidence range
under the Kass--Raftery convention \cite{Kass_Raftery_1995}.
That analysis already makes SMBHB modeling a key caveat for interpreting
apparent new-physics preferences.  Here we make the caveat quantitative:
we isolate the local-density versus official-density evidence shift,
evaluate one physical environmental-SMBHB control and two predeclared
curvature surrogates on the same official scale, and test whether a public
cross-PTA spectral bridge breaks the resulting non-identification.

Recent public-data studies have pursued direct multi-PTA combination
\cite{FivePTA_2026}, Fourier-space reuse of posterior summaries
\cite{Valtolina_Haasteren_2025}, and Bayesian discrimination among
selected source classes \cite{Lai_Li_2025,Wang_Zhang_Xu_2025}.  Our
bridge is different: we anchor source identification to the official
NANOGrav/PTArcade evidence scale, calibrate cosmological templates
against curved-SMBHB controls on that same scale, and then test whether
the non-identification survives a deliberately limited cross-PTA
posterior-summary bridge.

Order-unity shifts in $\ln\mathcal{B}$ can arise from the SIGW kernel,
prior boundaries on peak frequency, or the density estimate used for the
free-spectrum likelihood \cite{Kohri_Terada_2018}.  We therefore
separate local ranking reproduction, official-density absolute-evidence
calibration, and a cross-PTA posterior-summary stress test.

\emph{Templates and likelihood.}
We model an isotropic stochastic background through the one-sided strain
power spectral density $S_h(f)$ and
\begin{equation}
\Omega_\mathrm{GW}(f)=\frac{2\pi^2}{3H_0^2} f^3 S_h(f),
\end{equation}
with $H_0=67.4\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}\,Mpc^{-1}}$.  The calibrated
NANOGrav official-template set includes SMBHB power laws, SMBHB
environmental hardening \cite{Sesana_2013,Sampson_2015},
SIGW-Gaussian, SIGW-delta, stable Nambu--Goto strings, cosmic
superstrings, and phase-transition spectra.  The cross-PTA bridge
additionally includes the PBH-SIGW analytic template and two
non-official cosmological controls listed in the Supplemental Material.
All $\Omega_\mathrm{GW}$ calculations are dimension-checked in
\texttt{code/gwb\_templates.py}.  The official-density comparison uses
only templates evaluable as stochastic spectra on the PTArcade
\texttt{ceffyl} density.

The calibrated evidence rows evaluate stochastic spectral templates on
released NANOGrav free-spectrum densities and on the official PTArcade
\texttt{ceffyl} density product.  ENTERPRISE \cite{ENTERPRISE_2019} is
used for Hellings--Downs chain validation and for the development
timing-level gates in the Supplemental Material, not as the production
likelihood for the official-density evidence rows.  In those validation
models, white-noise parameters are fixed to the official NANOGrav 15-year
noise dictionary \cite{NANOGrav_15yr_noise}, the common process uses 14
GWB Fourier bins at $f_i=i/T$, and timing-model files are not modified.
Model evidences are computed with \texttt{dynesty} nested sampling
\cite{Speagle_2020}.  The cross-PTA
bridge multiplies public PPTA, NANOGrav, and EPTA posterior-summary
spectral densities.  Its \texttt{hybrid3} version replaces only the
NANOGrav term by the official \texttt{ceffyl} density, so only
within-bridge $\Delta\ln Z$ values are interpreted.

\emph{Pipeline validation.}
Direct ingestion of the pre-sampled NANOGrav Hellings--Downs chain gives
$\log_{10}A_\mathrm{HD}=-14.20\pm0.13$ and
$\gamma=3.25\pm0.35$, matching Ref.~\cite{NANOGrav_15yr_GWB}.  An
independent Hellings--Downs PTMCMC chain \cite{PTMCMCSampler_2017}
gives $\log_{10}A_\mathrm{GWB}=-14.199\pm0.122$ and
$\gamma_\mathrm{GWB}=3.272\pm0.321$; tail-aligned warm+cold segments
remain below an a priori $\hat R<1.05$ convergence threshold.

\emph{Evidence calibration.}
All source-template comparisons below are conditional on the
HD-correlated stochastic-background hypothesis; we do not re-evaluate the
detection evidence for the HD spatial correlation itself.
Our local \texttt{ceffyl}-style refit starts from the released
Hellings--Downs 30-bin free-spectrum core, builds independent per-bin
KDEs for the 14 lowest bins, and evaluates each spectral template
through
\begin{equation}
\ln\mathcal{L}(\theta)=
\sum_{k=1}^{14}
\ln\mathrm{KDE}_k[\log_{10}\rho_k^\mathrm{model}(\theta)] .
\end{equation}
Since the released free-spectrum priors are uniform in
$\log_{10}\rho_k$ over the retained range, these posterior densities are
proportional to likelihood factors up to model-independent constants.
This local refit reproduces the qualitative NANOGrav ranking but not the
published absolute values.  The ablation below identifies the local density
construction as the main source of the absolute-evidence mismatch.

The released Hellings--Downs free-spectrum covariance is nearly diagonal.
Covariance restoration shifts the leading SIGW-Gaussian versus SMBHB
projection by only $-0.006$ nat, although it can be order-unity for spectra
far from the data, such as the stable-string proxy.  It therefore does not
explain the multi-nat local-vs-official evidence mismatch for the leading
comparisons.

The decisive test uses the official NANOGrav/PTArcade model files and
the official PTArcade \texttt{ceffyl} density product.  PTArcade model
files return $h^2\Omega_\mathrm{GW}$, so the residual conversion is
\begin{equation}
\rho^2=\frac{(H_0/h)^2\,h^2\Omega_\mathrm{GW}}
{8\pi^4 f^5 T}.
\end{equation}
This is the PTArcade \texttt{ceffyl} stochastic-spectrum convention used to
map $h^2\Omega_\mathrm{GW}$ into the residual-density coordinate.
Dimensional and code-level conversion checks are reported in the
Supplemental Material.

Figure~\ref{fig:evidence} and Table~\ref{tab:lnb} show sub-nat
official-density reproduction for the three published cosmological
templates; the largest absolute residual across the five configurations
is $0.694$ nat for SIGW-Gaussian.  In contrast, replacing only the
SIGW-Gaussian spectrum while retaining the local scipy-KDE density gives
$\ln\mathcal{B}=+0.687\pm0.147$, still low by $3.36$ nats.  Absolute
evidence alignment is therefore controlled primarily by official density
construction and template fidelity, not by bin covariance.
The curved-control classes and their prior variants were specified before
the official-density sweep; all tested variants are reported in the
Supplemental Material.

The same matched evidence scale changes the physical interpretation.
Curved-SMBHB controls are not a single-model accident:
eccentricity-inspired, smooth broken-power-law, and environmental
turnover parameterizations give five-configuration means
$3.839\pm0.043$, $3.656\pm0.154$, and $3.453\pm0.062$,
respectively.  The best phenomenological curvature surrogate lies only
$0.681$ nat below SIGW-Gaussian and $0.091$ nat below SIGW-delta.  The
physical environmental-SMBHB control lies $1.067$ nat below SIGW-Gaussian,
$0.476$ nat below SIGW-delta, and $0.104$ nat below superstrings.  These
gaps are comparable to identified calibration and prior-boundary
systematics, so they should not be interpreted as robust source
discrimination.  Independent prior-space
Sobol thermodynamic integration agrees with the dynesty robust means to
within $0.12$ nat for the leading cosmological templates and within
$0.063$ nat for the tested curved-SMBHB representatives.

\emph{Cross-PTA bridge.}
We next ask whether adding public PPTA and EPTA spectral information
breaks the NANOGrav degeneracy.  All 11 bridge templates passed the
local posterior-summary adapters and the NANOGrav official-density
likelihood-evaluation adapter; only the templates marked in the
Supplemental Material are official PTArcade spectrum implementations.
This is an anchored ranking stress test, not a fully calibrated
multi-PTA Bayes-factor scale.  We also staged timing-level and LSS
production gates.  Because neither gate has yet reproduced its required
public baseline, neither is used for evidence in this Letter; details are
given in the Supplemental Material.  In
the local3 bridge the leading tested
templates are cosmic
superstrings, SIGW-delta, PBH-SIGW, SIGW-Gaussian, eccentricity-inspired
SMBHB curvature, broken-power-law SMBHB curvature, and environmental
SMBHB turnover, with the three curved-SMBHB controls within $0.933$ nat
of the leader.  In the hybrid3 bridge, where the NANOGrav term is the
official PTArcade \texttt{ceffyl} density and the ranking is reported as
within-bridge $\Delta\ln Z$, the same qualitative tier persists:
SIGW-delta, PBH-SIGW, SIGW-Gaussian,
eccentricity-inspired SMBHB curvature, broken-power-law SMBHB curvature,
and environmental SMBHB turnover lie $0.092$, $0.173$, $0.472$,
$0.663$, $0.832$, and $0.939$ nat below cosmic superstrings,
respectively.

The sequential anchored ablation gives the same warning against
over-interpreting the bridge leader.  PBH-SIGW analytic leads the
NG15-official-only, NG15-official+PPTA-local, and
NG15-official+EPTA-local bridges, while cosmic superstrings leads only
after both public local terms are included.  In the full \texttt{hybrid3}
stress test, the leading tested spectrum and all three curved-SMBHB
controls remain compressed into an order-unity interval.

Figure~\ref{fig:bridge} also shows the tested-representative family
mixture.  This is an equal-weight mixture over the tested representatives,
not a complete marginalization over all physical realizations of a source
class.  It gives
$\ln Z_\mathrm{family}=-154.610$ for SIGW-like spectra and
$-155.182$ for curved SMBHB spectra, a separation of only $0.572$ nat.
Other cosmological spectra sit at $-155.279$, and simple SMBHB spectra
at $-155.650$.  The bridge therefore strengthens the physical message
rather than replacing it with a new source claim: current public PTA
spectral information identifies a broad class of low-frequency-curved
spectra, but not a unique astrophysical or cosmological origin.

The Supplemental Material gives the full official-density stochastic-model
ranking, curved-SMBHB family table, prior-boundary tests, and cumulative
low-frequency-bin scan, together with the cross-PTA bridge model list,
sequential ablation, tested-representative family mixtures, version and
plateau diagnostics, reduced timing-level development gates, and
robustness and final stability checks.  The relevant calibration,
prior-boundary, and bridge-robustness scales are summarized there and are
comparable to the remaining leading-model and family gaps.  In the
single-configuration
official scan the
leading six stochastic templates are SIGW-Gaussian, SIGW-delta, SIGW-box,
cosmic superstrings, metastable strings, and domain walls.  Extending the
SIGW-delta upper prior from $\log_{10}f_\mathrm{peak}\le -5$ to $-3$
increases its evidence by $0.426$ nat, a modest but non-negligible
boundary systematic.  Cumulative-bin evidence shows that SIGW-delta,
cosmic superstrings, eccentricity-inspired curvature, and environmental
turnover are already within $0.5$ nat of their 14-bin values by the
lowest eight Fourier bins, whereas SIGW-Gaussian and broken-power-law
curvature require ten bins.
Consistently, the three curved-SMBHB controls project to effective
low-frequency slopes $\gamma_\mathrm{eff}\simeq3.7$--$4.1$ across the
PPTA, NANOGrav, and EPTA frequency windows, flatter than the GW-driven
SMBHB value $\gamma=13/3\simeq4.33$.  This qualitatively matches a
window-dependent slope drift without implying a unique astrophysical
explanation.  A final posterior-summary diagnostic gives comparable
low-four-bin residuals for Astro-curved, SIGW-like, and other
cosmological families, with mean absolute residuals $0.462$, $0.465$,
and $0.478$ in LUT-standard-deviation units, respectively.  This is a
posterior-summary diagnostic, not a timing-residual posterior predictive
check.

\emph{Interpretation and conclusions.}
The calibrated NANOGrav evidence and the cross-PTA posterior-summary
bridge both point to the same conclusion: current public PTA data do not
yet identify the physical origin of the background.  On a calibrated
evidence scale, current leading new-physics preferences are compressed
into the same model-comparison tier as curved SMBHB spectra.  The
sharpest near-term
discriminant is spectral shape at low frequency.  For GW-driven SMBHB
backgrounds the reference scaling is
$h^2\Omega_\mathrm{GW}\propto f^{2/3}$, while environmental or eccentric
effects flatten this behavior at the lowest PTA frequencies.  By
contrast, peaked cosmological alternatives such as SIGW or first-order
phase transitions rise causally as $f^3$ below their characteristic
scale \cite{Caprini_Figueroa_2018}.  String-like spectra form a separate
near-degenerate branch in the current PTA band.  Our
cumulative-bin scan makes this quantitative: the lowest eight to ten
Fourier bins already determine the matched-scale ranking to within
$0.5$ nat for the leading stochastic templates.

The practical lesson is physical as well as methodological.  A local
free-spectrum KDE product is useful for fast diagnostics, but it is not
automatically equivalent to the official PTArcade \texttt{ceffyl}
density.  For absolute Bayes factors at the sub-nat level, the official
density product, exact template implementation, and matched SMBHB prior
baseline must be used together.  More importantly, cosmological
alternatives should not be interpreted as source identification until
they are calibrated against curved SMBHB controls.  On the calibrated
evidence scale, three distinct curved-SMBHB parameterizations occupy the
same model-comparison tier as the leading cosmological stochastic
templates; in the hybrid3 bridge the full leading set remains compressed
into an order-unity interval around the best tested spectrum.  The
remaining evidence gap is comparable to the calibration and
prior-boundary systematics identified here, so present public PTA data do
not yet support robust source identification between these leading
cosmological templates and
curved-SMBHB controls.
Free-spectrum bin covariance is a useful
diagnostic but is not the
dominant error budget for the leading SIGW/string comparisons.  This
claim is restricted to stochastic spectral-template comparisons
evaluable on the released free-spectrum density; PBH/ULDM templates exposed
only as time-domain \texttt{signal(toas,...)} objects require their own
likelihood objects.
